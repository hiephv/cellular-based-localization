We conducted experiments using two independent datasets collected on different days and in different areas to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
Dataset #1: Collected from April 4 to 8, 2025, in an urban area, with a total of 21,155 records sampled at regular intervals.
Dataset #2: Collected on October 9, 2025, in a different urban area, consisting of 1,470 records, also sampled at regular intervals.

Each record in the dataset corresponds to a measurement made by the mobile device. 
The format of each data record is:
(id, lat, lon, timestamp, n, LAC1, CID1, Lat1, Lon1, RSSI1, ..., LACn, CIDn, Latn, Lonn, RSSIn)

Where:
1. id: The index of the measurement (unique identifier for the measurement).
2. lat: GPS latitude of the device when the measurement was taken.
3. lon: GPS longitude of the device when the measurement was taken.
4. timestamp: The time when the measurement was recorded.
5. n: The number of cell towers (cells) observed by the device during the measurement.
6. LACi, CIDi, Lat_i, Lon_i, RSSIi (for i from 1 to n):
	- LAC: Location Area Code associated with each cell.
	- CID: Cell Identifier for each cell tower.
	- Lat_i, Lon_i: Latitude and Longitude of each cell tower, retrieved via services like OpenCellID, FindCellID, or Geolocation APIs.
	- RSSI: Received Signal Strength Indicator for each cell tower, which is used to measure signal strength from the cell tower.
	
Data Collection:
	- Environment: The measurements were collected using the mobile scanning application in the urban area of Hanoi City. 
	The environment is characterized by heavy vehicle traffic, people, and surrounding buildings, affecting signal propagation.
	- Application: The mobile device scans for GSM cell towers and reports relevant data about the towers’ LAC, CID, RSSI, and the device's GPS location.
	- Driving Method: Data was collected by driving around Hanoi City on a car or motorbike with the application running on the device.
